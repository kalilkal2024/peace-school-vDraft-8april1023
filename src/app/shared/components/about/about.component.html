Why Peace School?

Peace School is the first private school in rural area, Kanhchriech district, Prey Veng province Cambodia, established by Mr. Mam Sarann. It started to offer classes on 21 February 2022 and was recognized as a primary school by declaration letter from the Cambodian Ministry of Education, youth and sport, No.470, signed by H.E Dr. Hang Chuon Naron, Minister of Education, Youth and Sport of the Kingdom of Cambodia. The school was formally inaugurated in a ceremony on 25 December 2022 attended by Mr. Soeung Socheat , Governor of Kanhchriech district..

Educational Challenges in Rural Area

Education is a fundamental right, yet many children from impoverished backgrounds in rural Cambodia face significant barriers to literacy in both Khmer and English. In Prey Veng Province, Kanhchriech District, these challenges persist despite children attending public schools.

Several factors contribute to this issue. Overcrowded classrooms, with an average of 45 students per teacher in public schools, hinder individualized instruction. Additionally, half-day sessions restrict learning time, making it difficult for students to progress academically. Economic disparities further exacerbate the problem; the cost of uniforms, textbooks, and transportation makes education inaccessible for many families. Wealthier students can afford supplementary afternoon classes, widening the achievement gap.

Parental migration also plays a role, as many parents seek work in urban areas, leaving children without essential academic guidance at home. Rural schools struggle to attract qualified teachers, particularly for English language instruction. As a result, primary students often lack basic English literacy skills, while secondary students begin studying elementary English in Grade 7 without a solid foundation.

  • • Delivering engaging and effective teaching methods that enhance critical thinking, problem-solving, and communication skills.
  • • Creating a supportive and inclusive learning environment where students feel motivated, confident, and encouraged to reach their full potential.
